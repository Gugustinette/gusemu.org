# Android Devices

Emulation can be done on a wide variety of devices, from computers to smartphones, and even web browsers !

But if you want to reproduce a more authentic console-like experience, you might want to consider using a dedicated device for emulation.

Here are some popular Android devices for emulation:

- Single screen
  - [AYN Odin2 Portal](https://www.ayntec.com/products/odin2-portal)
  - [AYN Odin 3](https://www.ayntec.com/products/ayn-odin-3)
  - [AYANEO Pocket Ace](https://www.ayaneo.com/product/AYANEO-Pocket-ACE)
  - [AYANEO Pocket DMG](https://www.ayaneo.com/product/AYANEO-Pocket-DMG)
  - [Retroid Pocket Classic Handheld](https://www.goretroid.com/en-fr/products/retroid-pocket-classic)
  - [Retroid Pocket 4/4 Pro Handheld](https://www.goretroid.com/en-fr/products/retroid-pocket-4-handheld)
  - [Retroid Pocket G2 Handheld](https://www.goretroid.com/en-fr/products/retroid-pocket-g2-handheld)
  - [Analogue Pocket](https://www.analogue.co/pocket)
- Dual screen
  - [AYN Thor](https://www.ayntec.com/products/ayn-thor)
  - [Retroid Pocket Flip 2 Handheld](https://www.goretroid.com/en-fr/products/retroid-pocket-flip-2)

As these devices run on Android, they can access the Google Play Store, use your existing Google account, and do pretty much everything a regular Android device can do, but with the added bonus of integrated controls and a more ergonomic design for gaming.

However, we still recommend downloading emulators directly from the developers' websites, as the Play Store is often full of fake, untrusted and ad-filled emulators.
